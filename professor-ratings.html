Professor Ratings

Real reviews from brothers · Grades, difficulty, and what to expect

📊 How to read these: Difficulty and Quality are rated out of 5. Difficulty 1/5 = very easy · 5/5 = very hard. Grade shown is what the reviewer received.

ACCT 2001

Peters, Kimberly
Grade: A-
Difficulty: 4/5
Quality: 3/5
Textbook: Wiley Plus (required)
Attendance required. The tests test you on application — a lot more than just memorizing. To do well, really understand your homeworks and memorize study guides. Going to SI is helpful but not 100% necessary. The tests only get harder and the final is the hardest. Definitely hard to make an A+ but very easy to end with a B-. As long as you do your homeworks, study hard, and genuinely try, you will be fine. Mrs. Peters is the best teacher for this class — she fills out the study guide for you and is very helpful if you're struggling.

BIOL 1201

Luzan, Tatiana
Grade: A+
Difficulty: 3/5
Quality: 4/5
Textbook: Recommended (ebook with homework)
Attendance is not mandatory! Luzan is hilarious and honestly not a great teacher BUT you will pass if you put in any effort — she gives tons of bonus, you just have to study on your own. Would 100% take her if she's an option!

BIOL 2051

Brininstool
Grade: A-
Difficulty: 3/5
Quality: 3/5
Textbook: Online courseware
Brininstool is great! She takes attendance so don't skip — she has a Zoom option for attendance. Weekly Mastering Biology assignments. Tests are on Moodle taken at home and are fairly easy. However, lab midterm and final are VERY hard. They consist of all short answer so prepare early.

BIOL 2160

Crousillac, Scott (Review 1)
Grade: A-
Difficulty: 2/5
Quality: 4/5
Textbook: Yes
Homework and course load was very little. Attendance not mandatory. Quizzes were difficult but tests were straight from the slides and the test bank — not always exact but very similar questions. Not too hard of a class.
Crousillac, Scott (Review 2)
Grade: C+

Quality: 1/5
Textbook: Yes

Only grades are exams and quizzes so study at least a week in advance — tests are hefty and detailed. Only homework was quizzes — do this in groups and split up looking up the answers.
Hunt, Lyman
Grade: A+
Difficulty: 1/5
Quality: 5/5
Textbook: No
Attendance yes, but if you have to miss just email him — he is so chill. This is the easiest A ever. A midterm and final on Moodle. Every two weeks you have a 5 question quiz on Moodle so you don't go to class every other Friday. Answers are on Quizlet. Group projects are also super easy. Definitely not a stressful class at all. 100% recommend.

ENGL 2025

Tusa, Chris (Review 1)
Grade: A+
Difficulty: 1/5
Quality: 5/5
Textbook: No
This man is amazing. Even though it's a literature class, we only had to read 2 books and he made sure they had movies so we had the option of watching instead. We watch all movies/TV shows in class and you are graded for a reflection each week — as long as you put something down you get a 100. We watched Black Mirror episodes almost every time.
Tusa, Chris (Review 2)

Quality: 5/5

ENGL 2029

Turner, Michelle
Grade: A+
Difficulty: 2/5
Quality: 5/5

FIN 3351

Slawson, C
Grade: A
Difficulty: 3/5
Quality: 5/5
Textbook: Yes
TAKE HIS CLASSES!! Slawson is probably the best professor you'll have. He makes sure you know what's important for exams and truly wants you to learn the material. Attendance is required. He does a lot of "pop assignments" but grades them all on participation, not accuracy. He records all lectures and posts to Moodle. Homework load is really low. Grade breakdown: 25% HW, 25% pop assignments, 25% midterm, 25% final (gave the option to not take the final and keep your current grade). Didn't take his classes until last semester and regret it so much.

GBUS 3061

Thompson, Melissa
Grade: A
Difficulty: 3/5
Quality: 5/5
Textbook: No (Emergenetics purchase required)
One class a week. Attendance required. Communication-Intensive (C-I) course. Professor Thompson is amazing and super helpful — she really wants students to succeed. Includes writing your resume, elevator pitch, cover letters, LinkedIn, emailing professionally, interview prep, and a group project. The class ends a week before finals. If you're going into any professional setting (business, sales, marketing), take this class — I recommend senior year. Couldn't recommend enough!

Garello, Dominique
Grade: A
Difficulty: 3/5
Quality: 2/5
Textbook: Yes
She doesn't take attendance but does random polls in class for credit. There is a McGraw Hill assignment that takes forever due once a week plus a Packback. So much busy work. Only other graded assignments are the Midterm and Final — taken on Zoom with camera on but you could look at notes on another device. Not super hard, just very boring.

Fryling, Charles

Difficulty: 1/5
Quality: 5/5
Textbook: No
Attendance yes. All you have in the entire class is a midterm and final and everyone has the answers — there is an exact Quizlet for both. This class requires absolutely 0 effort and is the easiest 100 A+ you will ever make. Highly recommend.

PHIL 2020

Wells, Chris (Review 1)
Grade: A-
Difficulty: 2/5
Quality: 4/5
Textbook: No
Pretty interesting class. Going isn't necessary but it helps. He provides readings that are needed but some are hefty and harder to grasp — he does a good job explaining them though. There are regular online quizzes that help your grade a lot because exams can be tricky. He doesn't round up grades because he offers extra credit randomly throughout the semester. Overall good guy but a brain-stimulating course.
Wells, Chris (Review 2)
Grade: A
Difficulty: 3/5
Quality: 5/5
Textbook: No
I LOVE CHRIS WELLS! He uses an attendance sheet but is extremely understanding if you need to miss. He lectures well and provides lecture outlines on paper with everything you may need. There are some 10-point Moodle quizzes (simple if you have the sheet) and 2-3 exams that are online essays — not hard if you pay attention. Homework load is minimal. These classes teach you a lot about life and the way we live. Extremely interesting material and engaging lectures.

SPAN 2102

Breaux, Kim
Grade: B-
Difficulty: 4/5
Quality: 2/5
Textbook: Yes
She speaks only in Spanish so try your best to remember vocab and simple Spanish words. Do homework on time and come to each class knowing the topic or you will be lost. Quizzes and exams are hard — study Quizlets and the textbook for extra help. Attendance is important and sometimes she rewards students who show up on less crowded days.

SW 2000

Rizzuto, Jessica
Grade: A+
Difficulty: 1/5
Quality: 5/5
Textbook: Yes
If you're thinking about the social work field or just need an easy elective, take this class! Rizzuto is super sweet and really cares about her students. She's a full-time social worker and this is the only class she teaches. Attendance is mandatory but she cancels class at least once a week. All coursework is on Moodle at your own pace. All quizzes can be found on Quizlet or in the textbook. It's a service learning course so you do have to volunteer 4 hours at the food bank, but it's super easy.
